# Stacking Factors: Time, Price & Structure

<ConceptCard title="The Holy Trinity of Trading" icon="🏛️">
Every high-probability trade setup rests on three pillars: **Time** (when you trade), **Price** (where you trade), and **Structure** (what price does when it gets there). If any of these pillars are missing, the trade's probability decreases significantly.
</ConceptCard>

When discussing confluence, traders often mistakenly stack elements from only one category (e.g., stacking multiple price levels like Fibonacci, Support, and Pivot Points). True confluence requires stacking across the three fundamental pillars of the market.

## Pillar 1: Time (The When)

In algorithmic trading, time is often more important than price. The market does not move uniformly throughout the 24-hour cycle. Volatility and liquidity injections are heavily concentrated in specific windows.

**Key Time Factors to Stack:**
- **Killzones:** Are we in the London Open, New York AM, or New York PM session?
- **Macro Windows:** Are we in a specific 20-minute algorithmic macro (e.g., 9:50 - 10:10 AM)?
- **Time of Day/Week:** Are we trading on a Wednesday (historically the high/low of the week for many pairs) during the highest volume hours?

<Callout type="warn" title="The Dead Zone Trap">
A perfect Order Block formation on the 5-minute chart at 1:00 PM EST (during the lunchtime lull) has an extremely high failure rate compared to that same formation occurring at 9:30 AM EST. Price structures without volume backing them are traps.
</Callout>

## Pillar 2: Price (The Where)

Price dictates the exact level where you expect institutional algorithms to reprice the asset. We are looking for areas of extreme inefficiency or high liquidity concentration.

**Key Price Factors to Stack:**
- **Draw on Liquidity (DOL):** Is price magnetically drawn to a specific level (e.g., Previous Daily High)?
- **Fair Value Gaps (FVG):** Are there untouched inefficiencies in the market?
- **Order Blocks (OB):** Is there an institutional footprint left behind from a previous liquidity sweep?
- **Premium/Discount:** Are we buying below the 50% equilibrium of the dealing range?

## Pillar 3: Structure (The What)

Structure is the final confirmation. You have the right time, you are at the right price, but now you must wait to see *what* price actually does. This prevents you from trying to catch falling knives.

**Key Structure Factors to Stack:**
- **Higher Timeframe Trend:** Is the Daily/4H chart making Higher Highs?
- **Liquidity Sweeps:** Did price run stops before reversing?
- **Change of Character (CHoCH):** Has the lower timeframe structure shifted in alignment with the higher timeframe?
- **Displacement:** Was the shift energetic and violent, leaving behind a FVG?

## Bringing It Together: The A+ Setup

Let's look at an example of stacking these three pillars to create an airtight trade thesis.

1. **(Time) London Killzone:** At 3:15 AM EST, volume enters the market.
2. **(Price) Key Level:** Price trades down into a 4H Bullish Order Block that sits in deep Discount.
3. **(Structure) Sweep & Shift:** Price sweeps the Asian Session Lows (liquidity grab), violently displaces upward creating a 5-minute CHoCH, and leaves a fresh FVG.

In this scenario, you aren't just buying because "support held." You are buying because institutional volume (Time) entered at a historically significant discount (Price) to trap early sellers and shift momentum (Structure). 

<Callout type="tip" title="The Wait Protocol">
Never anticipate the third pillar. Many traders identify Time and Price perfectly, but enter *before* the Structural shift occurs. Waiting for the CHoCH and Displacement is your insurance policy.
</Callout>

<KeyTakeaways>
- True confluence stacks factors across Time, Price, and Structure.
- Time dictates volume and algorithmic participation.
- Price dictates the zones of inefficiency and liquidity.
- Structure provides the final confirmation that smart money has stepped in.
</KeyTakeaways>
