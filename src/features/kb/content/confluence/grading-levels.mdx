# Grading Levels: A+, B, and C Setups

<ConceptCard title="Systematic Execution" icon="📋">
Professional traders do not treat every setup equally. By grading setups based on the presence of confluence factors, you can dynamically adjust your risk and avoid taking subpar trades out of boredom.
</ConceptCard>

One of the greatest challenges in trading is the subjective nature of chart reading. What looks like a great setup on Monday might look terrible on Friday if you've had a bad week. 

To eliminate emotion, we must move from subjective feelings to objective grading. This is done by creating a rubric of confluence factors and scoring each trade before entry.

## 1. The Grading Rubric

A standard institutional grading rubric assigns a required checklist for a setup to be considered valid. Here is an example of an objective rubric based on the Time, Price, and Structure pillars.

**The Master Checklist:**
1. **HTF Alignment:** Is the trade in the direction of the Daily/4H trend?
2. **Time:** Is the entry forming during a Killzone (London/NY)?
3. **Location:** Is the setup forming at a HTF POI (Order Block, FVG, or major Liquidity Sweep)?
4. **Discount/Premium:** Are we buying in a discount or selling in a premium?
5. **Confirmation:** Did the LTF produce a clear CHoCH with displacement?

## 2. Defining the Grades

Based on the checklist above, we can categorize trades into three tiers.

### The A+ Setup (5/5 Factors)
The A+ setup is the "God setup." Everything aligns perfectly. You are trading with the overall trend, during a high-volume session, bouncing off a major higher-timeframe level in a discount, with perfect lower-timeframe confirmation.
- **Action:** Full risk allocation (e.g., 1% to 2% of account).
- **Frequency:** Rare. You might only see 1 or 2 of these per week per asset.

### The B Setup (3-4/5 Factors)
This is your bread and butter. The setup is highly probable, but perhaps one factor is missing. For example, the setup forms perfectly during the NY Killzone with great LTF confirmation, but you are trading counter-trend to the Daily chart (a retracement play).
- **Action:** Reduced risk allocation (e.g., 0.5% of account).
- **Frequency:** Common. You will likely see several of these per week.

### The C Setup (1-2/5 Factors)
These are the trades that drain accounts. A C setup usually happens when you are bored. For example, you see a CHoCH on the 1-minute chart in the middle of the lunchtime lull, nowhere near a HTF point of interest.
- **Action:** DO NOT TRADE. Skip entirely.
- **Frequency:** Unfortunately, these happen all day, every day, baiting impatient traders.

<Callout type="trap" title="The Boredom Trade">
The most dangerous setup is the C setup that you trick yourself into believing is a B setup because you haven't taken a trade in three days. If you have to squint to see the confluence, the trade isn't there.
</Callout>

## 3. Dynamic Risk Management

Grading your setups isn't just about filtering out bad trades; it's intrinsically tied to risk management. 

If you risk 1% on an A+ setup and 1% on a C setup, your risk distribution is fundamentally broken. You are risking the same amount of capital on a low-probability coin flip as you are on a high-probability institutional alignment. 

By tying your risk per trade directly to the grade of the setup, you ensure that your capital is heavily weighted toward your strongest edge.

<KeyTakeaways>
- You must create an objective checklist to remove emotion from your trade selection.
- A+ setups possess all confluence factors; risk full allocation.
- B setups are playable but lack a minor factor; reduce risk.
- C setups lack major pillars like Time or HTF Location; do not trade them.
- Dynamic risk allocation based on setup grade protects your capital from low-probability variance.
</KeyTakeaways>
