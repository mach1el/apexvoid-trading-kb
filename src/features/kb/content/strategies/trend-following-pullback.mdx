The trend-following pullback strategy is the bread and butter of directional trading. It relies on a simple premise: in a strongly trending market, counter-trend moves (pullbacks) are usually temporary exhaustion or profit-taking, providing a low-risk entry to join the dominant trend.

## Strategy Blueprint

**Required Regime:** Clean Trending Market (Higher Highs / Higher Lows for Uptrend)

<Callout type="warning" title="Failure Mode">
This strategy fails miserably in choppy or ranging environments. If you are buying pullbacks in a range, you are providing liquidity (buying) right at resistance.
</Callout>

### 1. The Setup
- Identify a clear, undeniable trend (price is respecting moving averages, creating clean structure).
- Wait for a retracement into a high-value area (e.g., previous resistance turned support, 20 EMA, or a VWAP band).
- Observe a decrease in volume during the pullback, indicating a lack of counter-trend conviction.

### 2. Entry Trigger
Do not place limit orders blindly at support. Wait for a reaction. Enter when price prints a reversal candlestick (like a hammer or engulfing bar) at the support level, signaling buyers are stepping back in.

### 3. Stop Placement and Management
- **Initial Stop Loss:** Placed strictly below the pivot low of the pullback. If this level breaks, the trend structure is compromised, and the premise is void.
- **Trade Management:** Scale out 50% at the previous swing high (the most logical area for price to stall). Trail the remaining stop behind new swing lows to capture an extended run.
