Breakouts occur when price aggressively breaches a well-established level of support or resistance. While buying the initial breakout can be lucrative, it is highly susceptible to "fakeouts" (false breakouts). The Breakout & Retest strategy waits for confirmation, offering a safer entry point.

## The Mechanics

When resistance is broken, the market psychology shifts. Traders who sold at resistance are now trapped (underwater). When price returns to that former resistance level, those trapped sellers close their shorts (which requires buying), and new buyers see value. This turns old resistance into new support.

### Volume is the Ultimate Validator

A breakout without volume is a trap.

## Strategy Blueprint

1. **Identify the Level:** Locate a clear horizontal resistance or support that has been tested multiple times.
2. **The Breakout:** Watch for a strong candle close through the level accompanied by high relative volume. *Do not enter yet.*
3. **The Retest:** Wait for price to naturally drift back to the breakout level on lighter volume.
4. **The Trigger:** Enter when you see a bullish reaction (on a smaller timeframe) at the new support level.
5. **Stop Loss:** Place the stop just below the breakout candle's low, or below the retest pivot.

<Callout type="tip" title="Patience Pays">
FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) will scream at you to buy the initial breakout candle. By waiting for the retest, you accept that you might miss some trades that go straight up, but you will save yourself from countless brutal fakeouts.
</Callout>
