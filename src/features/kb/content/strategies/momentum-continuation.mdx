Unlike standard pullbacks which retrace deeply to major moving averages, momentum continuation patterns occur in hyper-aggressive markets. The buying pressure is so intense that price barely pulls back; instead, it consolidates sideways or slightly downward in tight ranges before exploding higher again.

These are classically known as "Bull Flags" or "Pennants."

## Recognizing the Setup

The hallmark of a momentum continuation is the **Pole** and the **Flag**.

1. **The Flag Pole:** A massive, near-vertical surge in price on extreme volume. This indicates a structural shift, heavy institutional buying, or a major news catalyst.
2. **The Flag:** A period of tight consolidation. Price drifts lower or sideways on drastically reduced volume. It looks orderly, not chaotic.

<Callout type="warning" title="The Danger of Deep Flags">
If the consolidation (the flag) retraces more than 50% of the initial flagpole, momentum has been lost. It is no longer a high-probability momentum continuation setup. True momentum only pauses; it doesn't give back half its gains.
</Callout>

## Execution Rules

- **Entry:** Buy the breakout of the flag's upper boundary. Alternatively, buy near the bottom of the flag if you have extreme confidence in the momentum, but the breakout is safer.
- **Stop Loss:** Placed strictly beneath the lowest point of the flag consolidation. 
- **Target:** Measure the height of the flagpole and project it upward from the breakout point (Measured Move). Momentum trades are fast; do not overstay your welcome. Take profits aggressively into the next thrust.
