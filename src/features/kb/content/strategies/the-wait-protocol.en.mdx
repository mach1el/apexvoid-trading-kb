# The Wait Protocol

The most destructive lie in trading is that you must be in a position to be a trader. In reality, professional trading is 10% execution and 90% waiting. Cash is a valid, active position. 

The Wait Protocol is not a strategy for making money; it is the ultimate strategy for **keeping** it.

## When to Stand Aside

You must invoke The Wait Protocol under the following conditions:

### 1. Choppy / Unreadable Price Action
If you look at a chart and cannot immediately identify the regime (Trend or Range) within 5 seconds, the market is choppy. In chop, price overlaps heavily, there is no momentum, and moving averages flatten and cross repeatedly. Your edge here is zero.

### 2. High-Impact News Events
Trading directly into CPI, FOMC, or Non-Farm Payrolls is gambling, not trading. Spreads widen, slippage is extreme, and technicals are temporarily destroyed by algorithmic liquidity pulling. Wait for the dust to settle and the real trend to emerge post-news.

### 3. Emotional Compromise
If you have just suffered a severe loss, or strung together 3-4 consecutive losses, your objective judgment is compromised. The urge to "revenge trade" will surface. The Wait Protocol mandates a hard stop for the session.

<Callout type="important" title="The Mathematical Reality of Drawdowns">
If you lose 50% of your capital in choppy markets because you couldn't sit on your hands, you don't need a 50% gain to recover—you need a 100% gain just to get back to breakeven. Preserving capital is mathematically more important than making it.
</Callout>

## The Discipline Checklist

Before taking any trade, run it through this filter:
- [ ] Is the current market regime clear and obvious?
- [ ] Is my chosen strategy appropriate for this regime?
- [ ] Is there a tier-1 setup presenting itself right now?
- [ ] Are we clear of major macroeconomic news?
- [ ] Am I calm, objective, and trading the plan (not my PnL)?

If the answer to *any* of these is NO, invoke The Wait Protocol.
