# ATR-Based Stop Placement

Placing stop losses based on arbitrary pip or dollar amounts exposes traders to the risk of being stopped out prematurely due to standard market noise. ATR provides a dynamic framework for stop placement that adapts to current volatility.

## The Logic of Volatility Stops

If a market typically moves 15 pips in a 5-minute candle, placing a 10-pip stop guarantees a high probability of execution simply due to random walk variance. A stop loss should ideally be placed outside the expected noise threshold so that execution only occurs when the premise of the trade is invalidated.

<ConceptCard title="The ATR Multiplier">
  Traders typically apply a multiplier to the current ATR to set stops.
  - **1x ATR**: Tight, assumes immediate continuation. High risk of noiseouts.
  - **1.5x - 2x ATR**: Standard. Accommodates normal pullbacks while protecting capital.
  - **3x ATR**: Wide, suited for trend-following strategies aiming to capture major swings.
</ConceptCard>

## Implementing ATR Stops in Structure

While pure ATR trailing stops (like Chandelier Exits) are common, ATR is best used in conjunction with market structure.
