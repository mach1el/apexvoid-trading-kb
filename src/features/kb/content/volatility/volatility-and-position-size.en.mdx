# Volatility and Position Sizing

Constant volume sizing (trading the same lot size on every setup) ignores the changing risk environment. When volatility expands, fixed lot sizes expose a portfolio to exponentially higher dollar risk per trade.

## Risk Normalization

The objective of dynamic position sizing is risk normalization: ensuring that the dollar amount risked on any given trade remains constant relative to account equity, regardless of how wide the stop loss needs to be.

<ConceptCard title="The Sizing Formula">
  `Position Size = (Account Equity * Risk %) / Stop Loss Distance`
  
  If the Stop Loss Distance is determined by ATR (e.g., 2x ATR), then as ATR increases, the Position Size must decrease to maintain the same monetary risk.
</ConceptCard>

## Psychological Benefits

Adjusting position size based on volatility protects capital during chaotic markets. It also removes the psychological strain of facing massive PnL swings during high-volatility events. A 50-pip stop with 1 lot carries the exact same financial weight as a 25-pip stop with 2 lots.

<Callout type="warning">
  Failing to reduce position size when ATR doubles means you are effectively doubling your risk profile without conscious consent. This is a primary cause of account blowouts during regime shifts.
</Callout>
