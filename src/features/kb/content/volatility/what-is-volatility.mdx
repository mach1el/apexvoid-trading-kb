# What is Volatility?

Volatility refers to the degree of variation in a trading price series over time. In quantitative terms, it is often measured by the standard deviation of logarithmic returns. For active traders, volatility dictates the potential range of price movement and directly influences risk parameters.

<ConceptCard title="Historical vs. Implied Volatility">
  **Historical Volatility (HV)** measures past price fluctuations over a specific lookback period. It reflects what has already occurred.
  
  **Implied Volatility (IV)** is derived from the pricing of options and represents the market's expectation of future volatility. While IV is forward-looking, most direct technical analysis of price action relies on HV metrics.
</ConceptCard>

## The Dual Nature of Volatility

Volatility is often mischaracterized simply as "risk." While high volatility environments demand wider stop losses and smaller position sizes—thus representing elevated risk—volatility is also the source of trading opportunity. Without price movement, arbitrage and directional trades cannot yield returns.
