# Volatility Regimes

Financial markets do not exist in a steady state; they cycle systematically between periods of low volatility (contraction) and high volatility (expansion). Understanding which regime the market is currently in is crucial for selecting the appropriate trading strategy.

## Contraction to Expansion

Volatility is cyclical. Extended periods of low volatility almost inevitably lead to violent expansions. This occurs because low volatility environments encourage leverage buildup and tight stop placement. When a catalyst arrives, forced liquidations trigger aggressive directional moves.

<ConceptCard title="Characteristics of Contraction">
  - Narrow trading ranges
  - Mean-reverting price action
  - Low trading volume
  - Moving averages flattening
  - Bollinger Bands tightening (the "Squeeze")
</ConceptCard>

## Adapting Strategy to Regime

A breakout strategy that performs exceptionally well during a transition from contraction to expansion will incur heavy losses during a pure contraction phase (due to false breakouts). Conversely, a mean-reversion strategy excels in contraction but risks ruin during expansion.
