# Wyckoff Accumulation

Accumulation is the process by which the Composite Operator (smart money) absorbs the floating supply of an asset from retail traders before initiating a markup phase (uptrend). This happens at market bottoms and forms a specific, logical structure.

## The Events of Accumulation

Accumulation ranges can be broken down into specific events that map the psychology of the transition from a downtrend to an uptrend.

1.  **Preliminary Support (PS):** Heavy buying begins to slow the downtrend. Volume increases, but the trend continues down.
2.  **Selling Climax (SC):** Panic selling reaches its peak. Retail traders capitulate. Smart money buys heavily, creating a massive volume spike and a wide-spread down candle that often closes well off its lows.
3.  **Automatic Rally (AR):** Selling pressure is exhausted. Short covering and smart money buying cause a rapid, automatic bounce off the SC lows. This defines the top of the trading range.
4.  **Secondary Test (ST):** Price revisits the area of the SC to test the balance of supply and demand. If the SC low holds on lower volume, it confirms accumulation is taking place.
5.  **The Spring (or Shakeout):** A critical deception. Price drops below the trading range lows (the SC/ST support). This traps breakout sellers and shakes out weak hand buyers. Smart money aggressively buys this liquidity, snapping price back into the range.
6.  **Test of the Spring:** A higher low after the spring on low volume, confirming sellers are gone.
7.  **Sign of Strength (SOS):** A strong, high-volume bullish move that breaks the top of the trading range (resistance).
8.  **Last Point of Support (LPS) / Backup (BU):** Following the SOS, price pulls back to retest the broken resistance as new support. This is the optimal entry point.
