# Wyckoff Phases A through E

Both Accumulation and Distribution schematics are divided into five distinct phases (A, B, C, D, and E). These phases categorize the progression of the market cycle from stopping the previous trend to initiating the new one.

## The Five Phases

### Phase A: Stopping the Prior Trend
Phase A marks the exhaustion of the preceding trend. Supply and demand come into balance.
*   **Accumulation Events:** PS (Preliminary Support), SC (Selling Climax), AR (Automatic Rally), ST (Secondary Test).
*   **Purpose:** To halt the momentum of the downtrend and establish the boundaries of the trading range.

### Phase B: Building the Cause
Phase B is usually the longest phase. It is where the institution is actively accumulating (or distributing) their core position.
*   **Events:** Multiple STs (Secondary Tests) and swings within the range established in Phase A.
*   **Purpose:** To absorb the floating supply (or demand). Volatility and volume usually contract as Phase B progresses, indicating the absorption is nearing completion.

### Phase C: The Test (The Trap)
Phase C is the decisive moment of the schematic. It involves a deceptive move to check the remaining supply/demand and trap retail traders.
*   **Accumulation Event:** The Spring or Shakeout.
*   **Distribution Event:** The Upthrust (UT) or UTAD.
*   **Purpose:** To sweep liquidity and ensure the path of least resistance is clear for the upcoming markup/markdown.

### Phase D: Trend Within the Range
Phase D is the transition from sideways consolidation to the new directional trend. Price begins to show clear directional intent but is still partly within the boundaries of the original range.
*   **Accumulation Events:** SOS (Sign of Strength), LPS (Last Point of Support).
*   **Distribution Events:** SOW (Sign of Weakness), LPSY (Last Point of Supply).
*   **Purpose:** To confirm the outcome of Phase C. The market is now dominated by the Composite Operator in the direction of the new trend.

### Phase E: The Trend Unfolds
Phase E is the breakout from the trading range and the unfolding of the massive trend (Markup or Markdown).
*   **Purpose:** The 'Effect' resulting from the 'Cause' built in Phase B. The asset leaves the trading range behind entirely.
