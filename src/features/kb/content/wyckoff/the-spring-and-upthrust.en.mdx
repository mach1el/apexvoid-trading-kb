# The Spring and Upthrust (Liquidity Sweeps)

Within the Wyckoff methodology, the **Spring** (in accumulation) and the **Upthrust** (in distribution) are the most critical events. They represent the final manipulation by the Composite Operator before the real trend begins. 

In modern trading terminology, these events are commonly referred to as **liquidity sweeps** or **stop hunts**.

## The Mechanics of the Trap

Markets need liquidity to move. Large institutions cannot enter massive positions without counter-parties. If an institution wants to buy a massive amount of an asset, they need a massive amount of sell orders. 

How do they generate those sell orders? By pushing the price below a major support level.

### The Spring (Bullish Trap)
When price breaks below the established support of an accumulation range (the SC and ST lows):
1.  **Breakout Sellers:** Retail traders who trade breakouts see support failing and short the market (creating sell orders).
2.  **Stop Losses:** Traders who are already long have their stop losses placed just below support. A stop loss on a long position is a sell order.
3.  **The Sweep:** The smart money absorbs all this engineered selling liquidity to build their long positions, causing price to violently reverse back up into the range.

### The Upthrust (Bearish Trap)
When price breaks above the established resistance of a distribution range:
1.  **Breakout Buyers:** Retail traders see a bullish breakout and buy the market.
2.  **Stop Losses:** Traders who are short have their stop losses placed just above resistance. A stop loss on a short position is a buy order.
3.  **The Sweep:** Smart money sells their final holdings into this massive pool of engineered buying liquidity, causing price to collapse back into the range.

<Callout type="warning">
A true Spring or Upthrust should be followed by immediate rejection. If price hangs around outside the range and builds acceptance on high volume, it is a genuine breakout, not a sweep.
</Callout>

## Trading the Sweep

Many professional traders solely trade variations of the Spring and Upthrust. The setup is highly logical:
1.  Identify the trading range.
2.  Wait for price to pierce the extreme of the range.
3.  Wait for price to close back inside the range.
4.  Enter the trade targeting the opposite side of the range.
