{/* 1. Hook */}
Every beginner trader is obsessed with finding a strategy with a "90% win rate." They scour YouTube for indicators that never lose. This obsession stems from the traditional school system, where scoring 50% means you failed the test. In trading, scoring 50% can make you a millionaire, provided your winners are twice as large as your losers. The pursuit of win rate without factoring in payout size is the single greatest misunderstanding in retail trading.

{/* 2. Definition */}
Your trading edge is defined by the relationship between two metrics:
1. **Win Rate:** The percentage of trades that hit your take-profit versus your stop-loss.
2. **Reward:Risk Ratio (R:R):** The size of your average winner compared to your average loser. If you risk $100 to make $300, your R:R is 3:1.

The **Breakeven Curve** is the mathematical line that shows exactly what win rate you need to survive based on your R:R. 

{/* 3. Deep Explanation */}
## The Mathematics of the Edge

To determine if a strategy is viable, you must calculate its breakeven point. 
The formula for the required breakeven win rate is:
`Breakeven Win Rate = 1 / (1 + Reward:Risk)`

### The Inverse Relationship
If your strategy aims for a 1:1 R:R (risking $100 to make $100), you must win more than 50% of the time just to break even after spreads and commissions. 

However, if your strategy aims for a 3:1 R:R (risking $100 to make $300), the math changes drastically:
`1 / (1 + 3) = 1 / 4 = 0.25`
At a 3:1 R:R, you only need to win **25%** of your trades to break even. If you win 30% of your trades, you are wildly profitable, even though you are "wrong" 70% of the time. 

This is the mathematical proof behind the old trading adage: *"Cut your losers short and let your winners run."*

## Engineering Your R:R

You cannot force the market to give you a 3:1 trade. You must engineer it by finding structural setups that offer that asymmetry. 

This is where [Market Structure](/foundations/market-structure) and [PD Arrays](/ict/pd-arrays) become crucial:
1. **The Invalidation (Risk):** You place your stop loss just below a structural Swing Low. This is a hard, mathematical boundary.
2. **The Target (Reward):** You place your take profit at the next major liquidity pool (an Old High). 
3. **The Filter:** You measure the distance. If the distance to the target is not at least 2 times larger than the distance to the stop loss (a 2:1 R:R), **you do not take the trade.** 

You skip the trade. You wait for a setup where the structure natively provides a 2:1 or 3:1 asymmetrical payoff.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: The High R:R System (The Professional)

**Scenario:** A trader uses the [Optimal Trade Entry](/ict/ote) framework. Their strict rule is to only take trades that offer a 3:1 R:R.
- They take 10 trades this month.
- They have a terrible month and lose 7 out of 10 trades (a 30% win rate).
- **The Losses:** 7 trades × 1R risk = -7R.
- **The Wins:** 3 trades × 3R reward = +9R.
- **Net Result:** +2R profit.

**Walkthrough:**
Despite being wrong 70% of the time, the trader walked away profitable. Because their winners were three times larger than their losers, the math absorbed the losing streak effortlessly.

### Example 2: The Scalper's Trap (The Amateur)

**Scenario:** A new trader uses a 1-minute scalping strategy. They want to "secure profits quickly," so they take profit at 5 pips, but they use a wide 15-pip stop loss to avoid getting wicked out.
- Their R:R is 0.33:1 (risking $3 to make $1).
- Based on the formula `1 / (1 + 0.33)`, their breakeven win rate is **75%**.
- They take 10 trades. They have a "great" month and win 6 out of 10 trades (60% win rate).
- **The Wins:** 6 trades × 0.33R reward = +2R.
- **The Losses:** 4 trades × 1R risk = -4R.
- **Net Result:** -2R loss.

**Walkthrough:**
The trader was right 60% of the time—better than a coin flip! Yet they lost money. When your risk is larger than your reward, you must maintain a nearly perfect win rate to survive. When (not if) a losing streak hits, the account is decimated.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the "best" R:R ratio?**
A: There is no universal best, but the industry standard for discretionary day trading is a minimum of **2:1**. If a trade does not offer at least double the reward for the risk taken, most professionals will filter it out.

**Q: Can I just set my take profit 100 pips away to get a 10:1 R:R?**
A: No. The market does not care about your math. If the next structural resistance is only 30 pips away, placing your target at 100 pips is pure hopium. Your targets must be based on structural liquidity, not greed. If the structure only offers 1:1, you skip the trade.

**Q: Is a high win rate bad?**
A: Not at all! A 60% win rate with a 2:1 R:R is the holy grail of trading. But in reality, as your targeted R:R goes up, your win rate naturally goes down (because price has to travel further to hit your target, increasing the chance of a reversal). You must find the balance that fits your psychology.
