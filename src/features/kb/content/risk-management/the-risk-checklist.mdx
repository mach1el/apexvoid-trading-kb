# The Risk Checklist

Professional pilots don't rely on memory before takeoff; they use a checklist. Traders must do the same. In the heat of the moment, the brain will justify low-quality setups because it wants action. The checklist is the objective filter that overrides emotional impulse.

<Callout type="info" title="The WAIT Protocol">
Before looking at the checklist, ask yourself: **WAIT** (Why Am I Trading?). Are you trading because your edge is present, or because you are bored, angry, or trying to make back a recent loss? If the answer is anything other than "my edge is present," close the charts.
</Callout>

## The Pre-Trade Gate

Do not execute the order unless every single box is checked.

1. **Has the setup formed according to my written trading plan?**
   - No guesswork, no "close enough." It must match the blueprint.
2. **Is the stop loss placed at a structural invalidation point?**
   - The stop is placed where the idea is proven wrong, not based on arbitrary dollars or pips.
3. **Is the total risk exactly at or below my maximum cap (e.g., 1%)?**
   - Position size has been mathematically calculated based on the stop distance.
4. **Is the reward-to-risk ratio at least 1:2 to the *first* logical target?**
   - If you have to hope price breaks major resistance just to get a 1:2, it's a bad trade.
5. **Am I emotionally neutral?**
   - If your heart is racing before you even click buy, your position size is too big or you are emotionally compromised.

## The Cost of Breaking Rules

When you skip the checklist, you shift from operating as a casino to operating as a gambler.
