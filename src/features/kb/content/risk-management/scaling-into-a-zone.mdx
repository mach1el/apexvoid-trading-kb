# Scaling Into a Zone

<AnnotatedChart chartData={chartRegistry.SCALE} />

Trading is rarely about picking exact tops or bottoms down to the pip. Often, price will react somewhere within a structural zone—like a Fair Value Gap (FVG) or an Order Block (OB).

Because you don't know exactly *where* in the zone the price will reverse, you can **scale in** by placing a ladder of limit orders across the zone. This is known as Zone DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging).

<Callout type="info" title="Why Scale In?">
Instead of placing one large order at the front of a zone (which has a wider stop and smaller position size), or one large order at the back of a zone (which often gets missed), scaling in gives you a blend: a guaranteed entry if the front is tapped, and a better average price if the zone is fully tested.
</Callout>

## Zone DCA vs. Martingale

It is critical to distinguish between planned scaling in (Zone DCA) and toxic averaging down (Martingale).

  title="Planned Scaling vs Toxic Averaging"
