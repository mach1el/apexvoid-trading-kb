# Why Risk is Everything

Most amateur traders spend 90% of their time looking for the perfect entry pattern, the magical indicator, or the ultimate bias. They spend 10% (or less) of their time on risk management. 

Professional traders invert this. They know that edge is fragile and markets are random in the short term. The only thing you can control is your risk.

<Callout type="warning" title="The Gambler's Fallacy">
A strategy with a 60% win rate can still blow up your account if your losses are too large. Win rate means nothing without proper risk management.
</Callout>

## Survival Comes First

The first goal of trading isn't to make money. The first goal is to **not lose your chips**. If you lose your chips, you can't play the game anymore. 

Consider this:
- If you lose 10% of your account, you need an 11% gain to get back to breakeven.
- If you lose 20%, you need a 25% gain.
- If you lose 50%, you need a **100% gain** just to get back to where you started.

Drawdowns grow exponentially harder to recover from. This is the mathematical reason why risking 1-2% per trade is an industry standard for professional speculators.

## The Illusion of Certainty

When a setup looks "perfect," the temptation is to size up. This is a trap. 

There is no certainty in trading. Every trade is just a probability. A "perfect" setup can fail because of a sudden news event, a large institutional order, or just random noise. If you risk 10% on a "sure thing" and it fails, the emotional damage will often lead to revenge trading and a blown account.
