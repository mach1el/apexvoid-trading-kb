{/* 2. Definition */}
**R (Risk)** is the base unit of measurement in trading. It represents the exact amount of capital you risked on a single trade. If you risk $100, then 1R = $100. If you make $300 on that trade, you made +3R. 

**Expectancy** is a statistical calculation that tells you exactly how much money (measured in R) your strategy is expected to generate on average per trade, factoring in both your win rate and your average winners/losers.

{/* 3. Deep Explanation */}
## The Expectancy Formula

To know if you have a mathematical edge, you calculate Expectancy per trade. 
The formula is:
`Expectancy = (Win % × Average Win in R) − (Loss % × Average Loss in R)`

### Example Calculation
Let's say your trading journal shows the following over your last 100 trades:
- **Win Rate:** 40% (You win 40 trades, you lose 60 trades).
- **Average Win:** +2.5R (When you win, you make 2.5 times your risk).
- **Average Loss:** -1.0R (When you lose, you lose exactly 1R).

Plug it into the formula:
- Win Side: (0.40 × 2.5R) = +1.0R
- Loss Side: (0.60 × 1.0R) = -0.6R
- **Total Expectancy:** 1.0R - 0.6R = **+0.4R**

This means that *on average*, every time you click the "Buy" or "Sell" button, you are making +0.4R. If your standard risk is $100 per trade, every click of the mouse is worth $40, even if that specific trade results in a loss. 

If your Expectancy is a positive number, you have a mathematical edge. You just need to execute the system flawlessly over a large sample size. If your Expectancy is negative, you are slowly bleeding to death, no matter how "good" you feel about your charts.

## Thinking in Probabilities

Once you know your system has a positive expectancy (e.g., +0.35R), trading transforms from a stressful emotional rollercoaster into a boring, statistical casino operation.

1. **The Casino Mindset:** A casino does not care if the next player hits the jackpot on the slot machine. They know the machine is programmed with a positive expectancy for the house over 10,000 spins. You must view your trades the same way.
2. **The Micro vs. The Macro:** In the micro (a single trade), the outcome is completely random. It could win, it could lose. But in the macro (a series of 100 trades), the outcome is mathematically certain, dictated by your Expectancy.
3. **Execution is Everything:** Expectancy only works if you execute the system consistently. If you randomly move your stop loss or take profits early, you break the math. You are no longer trading a system; you are gambling.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: The Turtle (Low Win Rate, High R)

**Scenario:** A trend trader catches massive moves but gets chopped up often.
- Win Rate: 25%
- Avg Win: +4.0R
- Avg Loss: -1.0R
- Expectancy = (0.25 × 4.0) - (0.75 × 1.0) = 1.0 - 0.75 = **+0.25R**

**Walkthrough:**
This trader loses 3 out of every 4 trades. Psychologically, this is grueling. However, because their winners are massive (4R), they have a highly profitable positive expectancy of +0.25R per trade. Over 100 trades, they will net +25R.

### Example 2: The Scalper (High Win Rate, Low R)

**Scenario:** A scalper takes tiny, quick profits but occasionally lets a loser run.
- Win Rate: 70%
- Avg Win: +0.5R
- Avg Loss: -1.5R
- Expectancy = (0.70 × 0.5) - (0.30 × 1.5) = 0.35 - 0.45 = **-0.10R**

**Walkthrough:**
This trader wins constantly. They feel amazing 70% of the time. But mathematically, their expectancy is negative (-0.10R per trade). They are guaranteed to blow their account over a large enough sample size. They do not have an edge; they have an illusion.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How many trades do I need to calculate my true Expectancy?**
A: A minimum of 30-50 trades executing the exact same setup. Anything less is statistically insignificant noise. 100 trades is the gold standard for proving an edge.

**Q: My average loss is exactly -1.0R. Why would it ever be different?**
A: If you always respect your stop loss, your average loss should be very close to -1.0R. However, slippage (during news events) might cause a loss of -1.1R. Conversely, if you actively manage trades and cut a loser early when structure breaks before your stop is hit, your average loss might be -0.6R, which vastly improves your expectancy.

**Q: Should I aim for the highest expectancy possible?**
A: Yes, but realistically, any system that produces an expectancy above **+0.20R** is excellent. If you are generating +0.50R or higher, you are printing money.
