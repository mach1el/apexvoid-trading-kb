# Risk of Ruin & Losing Streaks

Risk of ruin is the mathematical probability that an account will be depleted to a point where trading can no longer continue (often defined as a 50% or 100% drawdown). 

Traders who risk 5% to 10% per trade don't blow up because they are necessarily bad at analysis; they blow up because they fundamentally misunderstand variance and the inevitability of losing streaks.

<Callout type="warning" title="The Law of Large Numbers">
If you take enough trades, a losing streak of 5, 10, or even 15 trades in a row is not just possible—it is a mathematical certainty. Your risk per trade MUST be sized to survive this streak without catastrophic psychological or financial damage.
</Callout>

## The Probability of Streaks

Let's assume you have a strategy with a 50% win rate. Many traders think this means they will win one, lose one, win one, lose one. 

In reality, a 50% win rate over 100 trades has a nearly 100% probability of experiencing at least one streak of 5 or 6 losses in a row.

## Managing the Streak

When (not if) you hit a losing streak, your psychology will be tested.

If you risk 1%, a 10-loss streak leaves you down ~10%. This is annoying, but recoverable. Your mindset stays relatively clear. You can objectively review your trades and determine if market conditions shifted or if you made errors.

If you risk 5%, a 10-loss streak leaves you down ~40-50%. At a 50% drawdown, you need a **100% return** just to break even. The pressure to "make it back" usually forces traders to increase risk or take subpar setups, accelerating the death spiral.
