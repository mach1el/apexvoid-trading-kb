{/* 1. Hook */}
If you went to buy a car that usually costs $50,000, and the dealer told you they had marked the price up to $75,000 due to "high demand," you would walk away. You refuse to buy at a premium. Yet, in the financial markets, retail traders do exactly this every single day. They chase massive green candles, buying assets at wildly inflated prices, only to watch in horror as the market immediately retraces. Smart money never chases. They only buy in a Discount, and they only sell in a Premium.

{/* 2. Definition */}
The **Premium/Discount Array** is a conceptual framework for evaluating the "expensiveness" of an asset relative to its recent trading range (swing leg).
- **Equilibrium (EQ):** The exact 50% midpoint of the swing leg. It is fair value.
- **Premium:** Any price above the 50% Equilibrium line. This is where smart money looks to *sell* (or short).
- **Discount:** Any price below the 50% Equilibrium line. This is where smart money looks to *buy* (or long).

{/* 3. Deep Explanation */}
## The Institutional Pricing Model

Institutions operate like wholesalers. They accumulate inventory (buy) when it is cheap (Discount) and distribute inventory (sell) when it is expensive (Premium).

When a market makes a massive, impulsive move upward, it establishes a new Swing High and a new Swing Low. This entire leg represents the new pricing paradigm. 
If an institution wants to add to their long position, they will not buy near the Swing High. They will withdraw their buying pressure, allowing price to fall. Once price crosses below the 50% Equilibrium line, the asset is officially "on sale." They will then look for institutional footprints (like [Order Blocks](/order-block/what-is-ob) or [FVGs](/smc/fvg)) resting in the Discount zone to execute their buy orders.

### Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)
While anywhere below 50% is technically a Discount, institutional algorithms frequently target a specific, deeper retracement band known as **Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)**. 
Using a Fibonacci Retracement tool drawn from the Swing Low to the Swing High, the OTE is defined as the zone between the **62% and 79%** retracement levels (often with the 70.5% level acting as the sweet spot). 

If you can find an Order Block perfectly nested inside the OTE band, you have found one of the highest-probability setups in trading.

## How to Draw the Array

1. **Identify the Leg:** Find a clear, impulsive price swing. It must have a defined Swing Low and a defined Swing High.
2. **Anchor the Tool:** Use your charting platform's Fibonacci Retracement tool.
   - For an Uptrend (looking to buy): Draw from the absolute bottom wick of the Swing Low up to the absolute top wick of the Swing High.
   - For a Downtrend (looking to sell): Draw from the absolute top wick of the Swing High down to the absolute bottom wick of the Swing Low.
3. **Set the Levels:** Configure your Fibonacci tool to only show the following levels: 0%, 50% (EQ), 62%, 70.5%, 79%, and 100%.
4. **Filter Your Setups:** Look at your HTF points of interest (Order Blocks, FVGs). 
   - If you want to buy, and your Order Block is sitting at the 30% retracement level (Premium), *ignore it*. It is a trap.
   - Only execute buy orders at POIs that exist *below* the 50% line.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: The OTE Alignment (Idealized)

**Scenario:** AUD/USD pushes aggressively from 0.6500 to 0.6600, breaking Daily structure.
1. You draw your Fib from the Swing Low (0.6500) to the Swing High (0.6600).
2. The 50% Equilibrium level is 0.6550.
3. You scan the leg for Order Blocks. You find two:
   - OB #1 is at 0.6570. (This is in Premium).
   - OB #2 is at 0.6530. (This is in Discount, specifically inside the 62-79% OTE band).
4. Retail traders buy the pullback at OB #1. Price bounces for ten minutes, then smashes through it, stopping them out.
5. Price drops into OB #2 at 0.6530. The institutional algorithms trigger. 
6. Price violently reverses and rockets to new highs at 0.6650.

**Walkthrough:**
By applying the Premium/Discount matrix, you successfully filtered out a Fake Order Block (Inducement). OB #1 was simply bait to generate liquidity to fuel the move down into the true Discount zone where the institutions actually wanted to buy.

### Example 2: The Shallow Retracement FOMO (Grounded)

**Scenario:** SPX rockets upward on strong earnings news.
1. You anchor your Fib and wait for a pullback into Discount (below 50%).
2. Price pulls back to the 38.2% level (still in Premium) and starts to consolidate.
3. You get FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) and decide to buy anyway, fearing price will run away without you.
4. SPX spikes up slightly, then rolls over and completely collapses, dropping deep into the 79% OTE level before finally catching a bid.
5. Your FOMO entry in Premium resulted in a massive, unnecessary drawdown (or a stopped-out trade).

**Walkthrough:**
The market will test your patience. Sometimes, in incredibly strong trends, price *will* bounce from a Premium level and run away without you. Let it go. The discipline of the PD array is that you only trade when the math is heavily skewed in your favor. Buying in Premium ruins your Risk:Reward profile.

{/* 7. Trap Callout */}
<Callout type="trap" title="The Fibonacci Magic Trap">
  The 62% and 79% Fibonacci lines are not magic. The market does not reverse *because* of the Fibonacci numbers. The market reverses because there is a resting pool of liquidity (an Order Block or FVG) located in a wholesale pricing zone. The Fib tool is just a visual ruler to help you identify where "cheap" and "expensive" are. Never trade a Fib level blindly if there is no structural POI aligning with it.
</Callout>

## Modern Framework Translations

- **SMC / ICT:** This entire framework was formalized by ICT as the "Premium/Discount Array." The OTE (Optimal Trade Entry) pattern is one of his foundational teachings.
- **Classic TA:** Classic charting relies heavily on Fibonacci retracements (38.2%, 50%, 61.8%). However, classic TA often advocates buying the shallow 38.2% retracements in strong trends, which directly contradicts institutional pricing logic.
- **Value Investing:** In traditional finance, this is simply the concept of "Margin of Safety." You do not buy an asset when it is overvalued (Premium); you wait for a market correction to buy it at intrinsic value or lower (Discount).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Where exactly do I draw the Fib anchor points?**
A: From the absolute extreme of the swing. For an uptrend, anchor at the very bottom wick of the move that started the displacement, and drag to the very top wick before the retracement began.

**Q: Can I short a market just because it is in Premium?**
A: No. In a macro uptrend, the market will spend a lot of time in Premium before it finally retraces. You only use the PD array to filter entries *in the direction* of your trend bias. In an uptrend, you wait for a Discount to buy. You do not blindly short just because it is in Premium.

**Q: What if price drops past the 79% OTE level?**
A: If it drops to 100%, it has swept the Swing Low. This could be a [Liquidity Sweep](/smc/liquidity-sweep), or it could be a complete trend reversal (CHoCH). You must wait to see if the candle closes below the 100% line before making a decision.
