{/* 1. Hook */}
Every trader knows the old adage: "Buy the dip." But how deep should the dip be? If you buy a shallow 30% pullback, you risk getting run over when the market decides it wants a better price. If you wait for a 99% pullback, you will miss 90% of the trends. Institutions solve this problem mathematically. They have defined a specific, algorithmic "sweet spot" where the probability of a reversal is highest, and the risk is lowest. 

{/* 2. Definition */}
**Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)** is a specific retracement zone found between the **62% and 79%** Fibonacci levels of an impulsive swing leg.
- **62%:** The entry threshold. The moment price crosses this line, it is in OTE.
- **70.5%:** The "Sweet Spot." This is the exact middle of the OTE band and acts as the primary institutional target.
- **79%:** The last line of defense. If price breaks heavily below this, the setup is usually invalidated.

{/* 3. Deep Explanation */}
## Why the OTE Works

As we discussed in the [Premium/Discount](/smc/premium-discount-eq) module, institutions refuse to buy in a Premium (above 50%). They demand a Discount. 

However, a 51% retracement is technically a discount, but it is not a *deep* discount. The algorithms that control modern price delivery are programmed to seek out the deepest pools of internal liquidity (Order Blocks and FVGs) before resuming the trend. 

By waiting for the 62-79% OTE band, you are accomplishing two things mathematically:
1. **You are filtering out Retail Traps:** Retail traders love buying the shallow 38.2% and 50% retracements. By waiting for OTE, you are letting the market stop out the impatient retail traders before you enter.
2. **You are maximizing your Risk:Reward:** Because the OTE is so close to the Swing Low (the 100% invalidation level), your stop loss can be incredibly tight. A tight stop loss means your Risk:Reward ratio explodes, often allowing you to capture 1:3, 1:5, or even 1:10 trades.

### The Honesty Note: Lineage
<Callout type="note" title="The Origins of OTE">
  While the term "Optimal Trade Entry" was coined by ICT, the concept is entirely rooted in classical Fibonacci theory. The 61.8% level is the Golden Ratio, utilized by traders for nearly a century. The 78.6% level is the square root of the Golden Ratio, heavily popularized by Scott Carney in Harmonic Trading (the Gartley and Cypher patterns). The OTE simply packages these classical deep-retracement metrics into a highly actionable, institutional framework.
</Callout>

## How to Trade the OTE

The OTE is a framework, not a standalone signal. Do not place blind limit orders at the 70.5% line. You must combine the OTE with [PD Arrays](/ict/pd-arrays).

1. **Find the Impulse:** Identify a strong displacement that broke market structure ([BOS](/price-action/bos)).
2. **Draw the Fib:** Anchor your Fibonacci tool from the Swing Low to the Swing High (for a long setup).
3. **Scan the OTE Band:** Look inside the 62-79% zone. Is there a Bullish Order Block? Is there a Fair Value Gap? 
4. **The Trigger:** Wait for price to drop into the OTE band and hit the Order Block.
5. **The Entry:** Enter when price hits the Order Block inside OTE, or drop to a lower timeframe and wait for a [CHoCH](/price-action/choch) inside the OTE band for extra confirmation.
6. **The Stop Loss:** Place your stop loss just below the 100% Swing Low.
7. **The Target:** Take partial profits at the 0% Swing High. Leave a runner for further continuation.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: The Perfect Alignment (Idealized)

**Scenario:** NAS100 (Nasdaq) breaks out aggressively at the New York Open, leaving a massive bullish leg.
1. You draw your Fib from the 09:30 AM low to the 10:15 AM high.
2. You look at the 70.5% OTE level. Sitting exactly on that line is an unmitigated 5-Minute Order Block.
3. You set an alert for the OTE zone.
4. At 11:00 AM, price drifts down into the 62% level. It continues lower into the 70.5% level, perfectly tapping the Order Block.
5. You enter long. Your stop is 15 pips away below the Swing Low. Your target is the Swing High, which is 60 pips away. 
6. Price violently rejects the OTE and smashes your target. You just secured a 1:4 Risk:Reward trade.

**Walkthrough:**
This is the holy grail setup. The mathematical Fib levels perfectly aligned with the structural Order Block. You bought wholesale, protected your downside, and let the algorithm do the heavy lifting.

### Example 2: The News Sweep (Grounded)

**Scenario:** EUR/USD is waiting for an interest rate decision.
1. You identify a bullish setup. You have an Order Block sitting inside the OTE band.
2. The rate decision is released. 
3. Price violently spikes down. It slices through the 62%, slices through the 70.5%, and slices through the 79%.
4. It wicks all the way down to 105% (sweeping the Swing Low) before immediately reversing and rocketing upward.
5. If you had a limit order sitting at 70.5% with a tight stop loss, you were slipped and stopped out.

**Walkthrough:**
The OTE cannot protect you from macro news events. During high-impact news, algorithms widen their spreads and hunt for deep [Liquidity Sweeps](/smc/liquidity-sweep). They will intentionally push price past the 100% level to take out retail stops before reversing. Never trade the OTE blindly during red-folder news.

<Callout type="trap" title="The Micro-Swing Trap">
  A common mistake is drawing OTE Fibonacci levels on tiny, insignificant swings on the 1-Minute chart during the Asian Session. OTE only works when it is drawn on a meaningful, impulsive leg of price action that broke structure. If the leg is tiny and choppy, the OTE levels mean nothing. Always favor OTEs drawn on the 15-Minute, 1-Hour, or 4-Hour charts.
</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Should I enter at 62%, 70.5%, or 79%?**
A: You do not enter based purely on the percentage. You enter based on where the *structure* is. If the Order Block is at 62%, enter there. If the Order Block is at 79%, wait for 79%. If there is no structure in the OTE, do not take the trade.

**Q: Can I trail my stop loss behind the OTE levels?**
A: No. OTE levels are entry zones, not support walls. Your stop loss belongs behind the structural Swing Low (the 100% level), because if that level is breached, the market structure has officially changed.

**Q: What if price only retraces to 50% and then flies?**
A: You miss the trade. And that is perfectly fine. The goal of trading is not to catch every move; the goal is to protect capital and only execute when you have a massive statistical edge. If price doesn't give you OTE, you enact the [WAIT Protocol](/strategies/the-wait-protocol).
