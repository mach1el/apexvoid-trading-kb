## Anatomy of the Judas Swing

The Judas Swing is the "Manipulation" phase of the AMD cycle (Accumulation, Manipulation, Distribution). It relies entirely on the retail psychology of trading breakouts.

Here is the mechanical sequence of a Bearish Judas Swing (which results in a massive down day):

1. **The Setup (Asian Range):** The market chops sideways for 6-8 hours. It establishes a clear Asian High and a clear Asian Low. Traders have placed their stop losses just outside this range.
2. **The Open (London Killzone):** At 02:00 EST, London volume enters the market. The algorithm initiates rapid buying.
3. **The Trap (The Judas Swing):** Price spikes aggressively above the Asian High. 
   - Early short sellers are stopped out (creating Buy orders).
   - Retail breakout traders enter long (creating Buy orders).
4. **The Absorption:** The banks need to sell. They use this massive flood of artificial retail Buy orders as counter-party liquidity to fill their massive Short positions.
5. **The Reversal:** Once the institutional short orders are filled, the artificial buying stops. The market instantly collapses, trapping the retail longs underwater and initiating the true downtrend of the day.

### Why "Judas"?
It is named the Judas Swing because it betrays the retail trader. It presents a beautiful, convincing breakout, only to immediately stab them in the back.

## How to Trade the Judas Swing

To trade the Judas Swing, you must wait for the trap to be sprung before you enter. You are the hunter waiting for the trap to snap shut on the prey.

1. **Establish Bias:** Determine the Higher Timeframe (Daily/4H) directional bias. If the HTF is Bearish, you are looking for a *bullish* Judas Swing to short into.
2. **Mark the Asian Range:** Draw horizontal lines at the highest high and lowest low that occurred between 18:00 and 00:00 EST.
3. **Wait for the Killzone:** Do not do anything until the London Open (02:00 EST) or NY Open (07:00 EST).
4. **Identify the Swing:** Watch for price to violently spike through the Asian boundary *against* your HTF bias. (e.g., if you want to short, wait for a spike above the Asian High).
5. **Wait for Rejection:** Do not front-run the swing. Wait for the candle to close. If it closes back inside the Asian range, leaving a massive wick (a [Liquidity Sweep](/smc/liquidity-sweep)), the trap is set.
6. **Enter on Confirmation:** Drop to a lower timeframe (1M or 5M). Wait for a [CHoCH](/price-action/choch) and an [OTE pullback](/ict/ote) to execute your trade in the direction of the true trend.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: The Classic London Short (Idealized)

**Scenario:** The Daily chart of GBP/USD is in a downtrend.
1. The Asian session creates a tight 20-pip range. The high is 1.2750.
2. At 02:30 EST, price explodes to 1.2765. Retail traders start buying heavily.
3. You check your HTF charts. At 1.2765, there is a massive unmitigated 4-Hour Bearish Order Block.
4. The 15-Minute candle closes at 1.2745, leaving a massive 20-pip wick that pierced the Asian High and tapped the 4-Hour OB.
5. The Judas Swing is complete. You enter short.
6. Price collapses through the London session, dropping 100 pips to sweep the Asian Low and continue the macro downtrend.

**Walkthrough:**
This is the textbook setup. The Judas Swing was not random; it specifically targeted the Asian High liquidity to pull price into a Premium HTF Order Block. Once the algorithm acquired the liquidity and mitigated the OB, it delivered the true trend.

### Example 2: The Double Judas (Grounded)

**Scenario:** EUR/USD is ranging on the Daily chart.
1. London opens and spikes above the Asian High. It rejects, and you enter short.
2. Price drops 20 pips, but then stalls heading into the NY Open (07:00 EST).
3. At 08:30 EST, CPI inflation data is released. 
4. Price violently spikes down, sweeping the Asian Low, hitting your take-profit (if you were lucky) or instantly reversing.
5. It then rockets back up, stopping out your breakeven stop, and takes out the London High.

**Walkthrough:**
When the macro trend is unclear (ranging), both London and NY can perform their own independent Judas Swings on opposite sides of the range. NY will often use high-impact news to sweep the liquidity that London left behind. If you are trading a London Judas Swing, always take partial profits before the NY Open to protect yourself from the "Double Judas."

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does the Judas Swing happen every day?**
A: No. It happens roughly 60-70% of the time. On days with massive, one-sided momentum (trend days), price will simply open and run in one direction without ever looking back to manipulate the Asian range.

**Q: How big is the Judas Swing usually?**
A: In Forex (EUR/USD, GBP/USD), the Judas Swing is typically 15 to 30 pips above/below the Asian range. In Gold or Indices, it can be significantly larger due to higher baseline volatility.

**Q: Can the NY session have a Judas Swing?**
A: Yes! The NY Judas Swing typically occurs at 08:30 EST (due to news) or 09:30 EST (the equities open). It will often sweep the low or high established by the London session before reversing.
