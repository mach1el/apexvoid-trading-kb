Human beings are wired to seek simplicity. When an amateur trader places a limit order on EUR/USD, they don't place it at 1.09873; they place it at 1.1000. This psychological quirk creates massive walls of capital that dictate where trends die and reverse.

{/* 2. Definition */}
**Round Numbers** (also known as Psychological Levels, Big Figures, or Whole Numbers) are price levels ending in multiple zeros (e.g., 1.1000 in Forex, or $4,000 on the S&P 500). They act as natural support and resistance because they are universally identifiable and heavily traded by both retail and institutional participants.

{/* 3. Deep Explanation */}
## The Psychology of Big Figures

The power of round numbers stems from the way human brains process information. It is cognitively easier to anchor value to a clean, even number. This phenomenon is known as "digit preference" in behavioral economics. 

In trading, this manifests in order flow:
- **Retail Take Profits:** A trader buying Bitcoin at $43,000 is highly likely to set their Take Profit target at exactly $50,000, not $49,612. 
- **Retail Stop Losses:** A trader buying a stock at $105 will often place their Stop Loss just below the psychological $100 barrier, assuming it will act as a floor.

This concentration of orders creates intense volatility around these levels.

### The Institutional Mechanism (Option Barriers)

It is a misconception that round numbers only work because "dumb retail money" trades them. Institutions are actually the primary defenders of round numbers, particularly in the FX and Options markets.

Major banks write (sell) massive exotic option contracts (like barrier options) that expire worthless if the underlying asset breaches a specific price level. These barriers are almost exclusively placed at major Big Figures (e.g., 1.1000, 1.2000). 

If price approaches 1.1000, the bank that wrote the option stands to lose millions if the level breaks. To defend their position, the bank will aggressively sell the asset just *before* it hits 1.1000, creating massive institutional resistance. This is why you often see price approach a major round number, stall out 5-10 pips away, and violently reverse.

### The Hierarchy of Round Numbers

Not all round numbers are created equal. They follow a strict hierarchy of importance:
1. **The Double Century (000):** The most powerful. Examples: 1.1000, $5,000. These are macro inflection points.
2. **The Mid-Figure (50):** The secondary level. Examples: 1.0550, $4,050. These often act as intermediate support/resistance within a daily range.
3. **The Quarter-Figures (25 / 75):** Minor psychological levels. Useful for scalping, but rarely turn a macro trend.

## How to Trade Round Numbers (Step-by-Step)

Trading round numbers blindly is dangerous. You must use them as *confluence* areas, not standalone signals.

1. **Draw the Grid:** On your charting platform, enable a price grid or draw horizontal lines manually at the major "00" and "50" levels for your asset.
2. **Identify the Trend:** Determine the prevailing macro trend. If the trend is bullish, you are looking for price to pull back and find *support* at a round number.
3. **Wait for the Approach:** Watch how price behaves as it gets within 10-20 pips/points of the big figure. Does momentum accelerate (a potential breakout) or decelerate (a potential rejection)?
4. **The Sweep or The Squeeze:** 
   - *The Sweep:* Price spikes through the round number by 10-15 pips, triggers retail stop-losses, and immediately rejects back below/above it. This is a high-probability reversal setup.
   - *The Squeeze:* Price consolidates extremely tightly just *under* resistance (e.g., at 1.0990) refusing to drop. This implies buyers are absorbing the institutional defense. A violent breakout is imminent.
5. **Execution:** Enter the market only after the rejection is confirmed by a candle close, or the breakout is confirmed by a clean close above the level.

## Worked Examples

Let's look at the two most common ways round numbers dictate price action.

### Example 1: The Figure Sweep (Grounded)

**Scenario:** USD/JPY is in a downtrend and approaches the massive 150.00 level. 
1. The media is hyping up 150.00 as a "historic floor." Retail traders place thousands of buy limit orders exactly at 150.00, with stops at 149.80.
2. Price drops to 150.10, stalls, and then violently drops to 149.75.
3. The retail stops are triggered. The media claims the floor is broken.
4. Immediately, massive institutional buy volume steps in. The 4-Hour candle closes at 150.30, leaving a massive 55-pip rejection wick below the figure. 
5. Price rallies 300 pips over the next week.

**Walkthrough:** 
The 150.00 level was too obvious. The institutions knew the retail liquidity rested just below it. By driving price through the psychological floor, they acquired the liquidity necessary to reverse the macro trend. You never place a limit order exactly *on* the figure; you wait for the sweep and buy the rejection.

### Example 2: The Vacuum Effect (Idealized)

**Scenario:** GBP/USD breaks through heavy resistance at 1.2930. 
1. There are no major structural levels (old highs) between 1.2930 and 1.3000. 
2. As price clears 1.2950, momentum suddenly accelerates drastically.
3. Price rockets in a straight line for 50 pips, hitting 1.3000 in minutes, where it immediately stops and pulls back.

**Walkthrough:**
This is known as the "Vacuum Effect" or magnetic draw. When price clears minor resistance and a major Big Figure is near, it acts like a magnet. Large algorithms recognize the lack of resistance and aggressively buy to push price into the liquidity pool resting at the round number. Traders use this to hold winning positions longer, knowing 1.3000 is the ultimate draw on liquidity.

## Modern Framework Translations

- **ICT:** Round numbers are referred to as "Institutional Pricing." ICT teaches that algorithmic price delivery specifically targets these levels (00, 20, 50, 80) as part of the Interbank Price Delivery Algorithm (IPDA).
- **Order Flow:** Heatmap software (like Bookmap) visually confirms this phenomenon. You will routinely see massive, bright red bands of resting limit orders clustered precisely on the "00" levels.
- **Price Action:** Classic support and resistance theory often uses round numbers as the backbone for drawing horizontal zones, acknowledging the psychological impact of whole numbers on crowd behavior.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do round numbers work on cryptocurrencies?**
A: Yes, incredibly well. Because crypto is heavily retail-driven, the psychological impact of big numbers is magnified. Bitcoin struggling to break $50,000, $60,000, or $100,000 is a perfect example of round number resistance.

**Q: How wide is the "zone" around a round number?**
A: A round number is not a razor-thin line; it is a zone. In Forex, the zone is typically 10 to 20 pips on either side (e.g., 1.0980 to 1.1020). Price will often interact within this buffer before reversing or breaking out.

**Q: What happens if a major round number is broken?**
A: If a massive level (like EUR/USD 1.1000) is broken and price *accepts* trading above it (closing multiple daily candles above), the psychology flips. The former massive resistance becomes massive support, and price often "vacuums" toward the next mid-figure (1.1050) or big figure (1.1100).
