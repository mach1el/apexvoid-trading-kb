{/* 2. Definition */}
**PDH** (Previous Daily High) and **PDL** (Previous Daily Low) are the extreme price points recorded during the prior trading day. **PWH** and **PWL** are the extremes of the prior trading week. These levels act as massive magnets for institutional liquidity.

{/* 3. Deep Explanation */}
## Why Do Previous Extremes Matter?

In the [Liquidity Intuition](/foundations/liquidity-intuition) module, we established that institutions need massive pools of counter-party orders (liquidity) to enter or exit large positions without suffering slippage. 

The single largest, most obvious pools of liquidity on any chart rest just above the Previous Daily High (PDH) and just below the Previous Daily Low (PDL). 
Why? Because that is where every intraday and short-term swing trader places their stop-losses. 
- If a retail trader bought yesterday and held the position overnight, their stop-loss is placed just below the PDL. 
- If a retail breakout trader is waiting to short the market today, they place a sell-stop order just below the PDL.

This creates an enormous cluster of sell orders below the PDL, and an enormous cluster of buy orders above the PDH. 

### The Institutional Raid (Sweep)

When the market opens the next day, institutional algorithms map these liquidity pools. If a bank needs to accumulate a massive long position today, the most efficient way to get filled is to drive the price *down* through the PDL. 

This action triggers all the resting retail sell orders (stops and breakout shorts). The bank absorbs all this selling pressure, filling their buy orders at a discount. Once their orders are filled, the artificial selling pressure ceases, and the true directional move begins upward. The PDL was swept.

### The True Breakout (Displacement)

Occasionally, price will push through the PDH or PDL and *sustain* that pricing, never looking back. This occurs when the macroeconomic narrative fundamentally shifts, or during powerful HTF trend continuations. In these cases, the liquidity above the PDH isn't just used to fill counter-orders; it acts as fuel to accelerate the trend (a short squeeze). 

However, statistically, a sweep and reversal is vastly more common than a sustained breakout, especially in range-bound or highly manipulated markets like Forex.

## How to Trade PDH/PDL (Step-by-Step)

1. **Mark the Levels at Midnight:** Before your trading session begins, open a Daily chart. Draw a horizontal line at the extreme high wick and extreme low wick of the *previous* completed daily candle. 
2. **Determine the Bias:** Look at the HTF market structure. Are we in a macro uptrend? If yes, your primary objective is to wait for price to drop and raid the PDL, so you can buy the reversal.
3. **Wait for the Raid:** Drop to a lower timeframe (e.g., 5-Minute or 15-Minute). Wait patiently for price to approach and pierce your PDL line. Do not front-run it.
4. **Look for Rejection:** Observe the candle close. If a 5M candle pushes below the PDL but violently rejects, leaving a long lower wick and closing back above the PDL, the liquidity sweep is confirmed.
5. **Execute on the Shift:** Enter the market when the lower timeframe structure shifts bullish (a CHoCH) immediately following the sweep, targeting the opposing side of the range (the PDH).

### Example 2: The PWH Trend Continuation (Idealized)

**Scenario:** We are in a strong macro Weekly uptrend. Last week printed a large bullish candle. 
1. Monday opens and price consolidates.
2. Tuesday pushes downward, raiding Monday's low (building a Discount).
3. Wednesday surges upward, smashing through the Previous Weekly High (PWH).
4. The break of the PWH triggers massive buy stops. Instead of rejecting, large momentum candles (Displacement) continue to print, closing far above the PWH.

**Walkthrough:**
Because the macro trend is intensely bullish, the PWH wasn't just a liquidity pool for a reversal; it was the trigger for a volatility expansion. The lack of a rejection wick at the PWH confirmed that institutions were sponsoring higher prices. The PWH transitions from a target to a support floor for the rest of the week.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What time zone should I use to define the Daily candle?**
A: For Forex and Crypto, the universally accepted standard is New York midnight (EST/EDT). Institutional algos reset and calculate daily ranges based on the New York close (5:00 PM EST) and midnight open. 

**Q: Do I use the wick or the body to mark the PDH/PDL?**
A: Absolutely the wick. The extreme wick is where the physical stop-loss orders are actually placed. The body tells you where the period closed, but the wick tells you where the liquidity rests.

**Q: If price sweeps the PDL, is it guaranteed to target the PDH?**
A: Nothing is guaranteed. However, if the HTF trend is bullish and the PDL is violently swept with strong displacement, the PDH becomes the highest-probability statistical draw on liquidity for that session. If the daily range is very large, targeting Equilibrium (the 50% mark) of the previous day is a safer first take-profit.
