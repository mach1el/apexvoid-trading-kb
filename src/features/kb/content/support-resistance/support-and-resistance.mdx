# Support and Resistance

Support and Resistance are foundational concepts in price action trading. They represent areas on a chart where buying and selling interest is historically strong enough to halt or reverse price movements.

<ConceptCard title="Support vs. Resistance">
**Support:** A price level where a downtrend tends to pause due to a concentration of demand (buying interest). It acts as a "floor" for price.
**Resistance:** A price level where an uptrend tends to pause due to a concentration of supply (selling interest). It acts as a "ceiling" for price.
</ConceptCard>

## How Levels Are Formed

Support and resistance levels are created by the psychology of market participants and the memory of previous price action. When price bounces from a specific level, traders remember it. If price returns to that level, buyers (at support) or sellers (at resistance) often step in again, anticipating another reaction.

<Callout type="info">
The more times a support or resistance level is tested and holds, the stronger it becomes in the minds of traders. However, eventually, heavy testing can weaken a level as the resting orders (liquidity) are consumed.
</Callout>
