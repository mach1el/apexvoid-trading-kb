# Regular vs. Hidden Divergence

Divergence comes in two primary forms: Regular and Hidden. While regular divergence indicates a potential trend reversal, hidden divergence suggests the continuation of the existing trend.

## Regular Divergence (Trend Reversal)

Regular divergence is used to identify the end of a trend. It occurs when price makes extreme highs or lows, but the indicator fails to follow suit.

*   **Bullish Regular Divergence:** Price makes a Lower Low (LL), but the indicator makes a Higher Low (HL). Indicates weakening downside momentum and a potential reversal to the upside.
*   **Bearish Regular Divergence:** Price makes a Higher High (HH), but the indicator makes a Lower High (LH). Indicates weakening upside momentum and a potential reversal to the downside.

## Hidden Divergence (Trend Continuation)

Hidden divergence is used to catch pullbacks within an established trend. It signals that the core trend is still strong despite a short-term price retracement.

*   **Bullish Hidden Divergence:** Price makes a Higher Low (HL), but the indicator makes a Lower Low (LL). Found in uptrends, indicating the pullback is exhausted and the uptrend will resume.
*   **Bearish Hidden Divergence:** Price makes a Lower High (LH), but the indicator makes a Higher High (HH). Found in downtrends, indicating the rally is exhausted and the downtrend will resume.
