# Glossary of Terms

<ConceptCard title="The Language of the Markets" icon="📖">
Trading is full of acronyms and jargon that can overwhelm new traders. Use this glossary as your translation guide when navigating different methodologies.
</ConceptCard>

## A

**Algorithmic Trading (Algos)**
Automated trading systems used by institutions to execute large orders incrementally without disrupting market prices. Often responsible for creating precise structural patterns like FVGs and OTEs.

**Asian Session**
The trading period primarily covering Tokyo and Sydney hours. Often characterized by lower volatility and consolidation, creating a "range" that is frequently swept for liquidity in the subsequent London session.

## B

**Bias**
The trader's directional hypothesis (Bullish or Bearish) derived from Higher Timeframe (HTF) analysis.

**BOS (Break of Structure)**
A continuation pattern where price breaks and closes beyond a previous swing high (in an uptrend) or swing low (in a downtrend), confirming the ongoing trend.

**BSL (Buy-Side Liquidity)**
Pools of buy-stop orders resting above previous highs (e.g., PDH, Swing Highs). Institutions trigger these stops to generate the liquidity needed to execute large short positions.

## C

**CHoCH (Change of Character)**
The first structural signal of a potential trend reversal. Occurs when a minor internal lower high is broken in a downtrend (or higher low in an uptrend), suggesting momentum is shifting.

**Confluence**
The alignment of multiple, independent technical factors (Time, Price, and Structure) at a single zone, increasing the probability of a successful trade.

## D

**Displacement**
A violent, high-momentum move in price characterized by large-bodied candles that close near their extremes. Displacement often leaves behind Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) and validates Order Blocks.

**DOL (Draw on Liquidity)**
The magnetic price level that the market is currently reaching for. Markets always move from internal liquidity (FVGs) to external liquidity (BSL/SSL).

**Drawdown (DD)**
The peak-to-trough decline of a trader's account capital. Minimizing drawdown is mathematically imperative for survival.

## E

**Equilibrium (EQ)**
The exact 50% midpoint of a defined dealing range. Professional traders prefer to buy below EQ (Discount) and sell above EQ (Premium).

**Expectancy**
The mathematical formula that calculates the average amount a trader can expect to win (or lose) per trade, factoring in both Win Rate and Risk-to-Reward Ratio.

## F

**FVG (Fair Value Gap)**
An inefficiency in price delivery caused by rapid displacement. It appears as a three-candle sequence where the wicks of the first and third candles do not overlap, leaving a "gap" in the body of the second candle.

## H

**HTF (Higher Timeframe)**
The chart timeframe used to determine the macro trend and directional bias. (e.g., Daily or 4-Hour chart).

## I

**Imbalance**
See *Fair Value Gap (FVG)*. A zone where aggressive buying or selling caused a lack of two-sided trading.

**ITF (Intermediate Timeframe)**
The chart timeframe used to locate specific Points of Interest (POIs) aligned with the HTF bias. (e.g., 1-Hour chart).

## K

**Killzone**
Specific time windows (usually defined by ICT) characterized by high algorithmic volume and volatility, typically occurring around the opening of major market sessions (London Open, NY Open).

## L

**Liquidity Sweep (Stop Hunt)**
A purposeful move by institutional algorithms to briefly push price beyond a key structural level (triggering retail stop losses) before reversing direction.

**LTF (Lower Timeframe)**
The chart timeframe used for precise execution and risk mitigation (e.g., 1-Minute or 5-Minute chart).

## O

**OB (Order Block)**
The last down-close candle before a bullish displacement (or last up-close candle before a bearish displacement). It represents the footprint of institutional limit orders absorbing retail flow.

**OTE (Optimal Trade Entry)**
A specific Fibonacci retracement zone (typically between the 62% and 79% levels) where price offers a deep, favorable discount before continuing its trend.

## P

**PDH / PDL (Previous Daily High / Low)**
The highest and lowest price points of the preceding day. These are major targets for liquidity sweeps.

**POI (Point of Interest)**
A specific price zone on a chart (like an Order Block or FVG) where a trader expects a reaction and waits for lower-timeframe confirmation.

**Premium / Discount**
Pricing concepts relative to a dealing range's Equilibrium (50%). Premium (above 50%) is where you want to sell/short. Discount (below 50%) is where you want to buy/long.

## R

**R:R (Risk-to-Reward Ratio)**
The amount of capital risked on a trade compared to the potential profit. A 1:3 R:R means risking $1 to make $3.

**R-Multiple**
A unit of measurement representing the initial risk taken on a trade. Making "2R" means doubling your initial risked amount.

## S

**SMC (Smart Money Concepts)**
An analytical framework that attempts to track the institutional algorithms ("Smart Money") by focusing on liquidity manipulation, Order Blocks, and inefficiencies.

**SSL (Sell-Side Liquidity)**
Pools of sell-stop orders resting below previous lows. Institutions trigger these stops to generate the liquidity needed to execute large long positions.
