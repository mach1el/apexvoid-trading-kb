# HTF → LTF Workflow

<ConceptCard title="Top-Down Analysis" icon="🔭">
Top-Down Analysis is the practice of determining the overarching market direction on a Higher Timeframe (HTF), locating a specific zone of interest on an Intermediate Timeframe (ITF), and executing the trade on a Lower Timeframe (LTF).
</ConceptCard>

If you open a 5-minute chart without knowing what the Daily chart looks like, you are driving blindfolded. The lower timeframes are noisy, erratic, and full of traps. The higher timeframes dictate the true institutional flow of capital. 

The HTF → LTF workflow is how we align our micro entries with macro capital flows.

## The Timeframe Triad

Professional traders typically use a triad of timeframes. The specific timeframes depend on your style (Day Trader vs. Swing Trader), but the relationship remains the same.

1. **The Bias Timeframe (HTF):** Dictates direction. You only trade in this direction.
2. **The POI Timeframe (ITF):** Dictates location. Where will you look for an entry?
3. **The Entry Timeframe (LTF):** Dictates execution. When exactly do you pull the trigger?

### Common Triads
- **Day Trader:** Daily (Bias) → 1H (POI) → 5m (Entry)
- **Scalper:** 4H (Bias) → 15m (POI) → 1m (Entry)
- **Swing Trader:** Weekly (Bias) → Daily (POI) → 4H (Entry)

## Step 1: The HTF Bias (Direction)

Start your day by looking at your HTF (e.g., Daily chart). Your only goal here is to answer two questions:
1. What is the current market structure (Bullish, Bearish, Ranging)?
2. Where is the next major Draw on Liquidity (DOL)?

If the Daily chart is making Higher Highs and the next obvious liquidity pool is a prominent swing high above current price, your bias is **Bullish**. For the rest of the day, you are generally only looking for long setups.

## Step 2: The ITF POI (Location)

Drop down to your Intermediate Timeframe (e.g., 1H chart). Now that you know you want to buy, you need to find *where* institutional algorithms are likely to offer a discount.

You are looking for Points of Interest (POIs) that align with your bullish bias. This could be:
- An untested 1H Bullish Order Block.
- A 1H Fair Value Gap (FVG) resting in a Discount array.
- A sweep of the previous session's lows.

Mark these zones on your chart. **Do not execute yet.** Set alerts at these zones and wait.

<Callout type="trap" title="The Premature Entry Trap">
Setting a blind limit order at an ITF POI is dangerous. A 1H Order Block is just an idea. It might hold, or price might smash right through it. You must drop to the LTF for confirmation.
</Callout>

## Step 3: The LTF Entry (Execution)

When your alert fires and price enters your 1H POI, drop to your Lower Timeframe (e.g., 5m or 1m chart). 

You are now watching price action inside the "kill box." You do not buy just because price touched the zone. You wait for the LTF structure to shift in your favor. 

**The Execution Trigger:**
1. Wait for a LTF Liquidity Sweep inside the HTF POI.
2. Wait for a LTF Change of Character (CHoCH) with energetic displacement.
3. Wait for price to pull back into the newly created LTF Order Block or FVG.
4. Execute the trade. Place your stop loss below the LTF swing low.

## The Power of the Workflow

Why go through all this effort? **Risk to Reward (R:R).**

If you trade directly off the Daily chart, your stop loss might need to be 100 pips wide. By framing the idea on the Daily chart, finding the zone on the 1H, and executing on the 1m, you can reduce your stop loss to 5 pips. 

When your 5-pip risk catches a move dictated by the Daily trend, your Risk to Reward ratio becomes exponential (e.g., 1:10 or 1:20 R:R). This asymmetry is how fortunes are built.

<KeyTakeaways>
- Never execute on a LTF without HTF context.
- The HTF gives you direction; the ITF gives you location; the LTF gives you confirmation.
- Wait for price to come to your POIs. Do not chase price in the middle of a range.
- Top-Down Analysis allows for extremely tight stop losses on macro-driven moves, unlocking asymmetric R:R.
</KeyTakeaways>
