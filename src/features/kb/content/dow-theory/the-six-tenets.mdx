# The Six Tenets of Dow Theory

<ConceptCard title="The Bedrock of Technical Analysis" icon="🏛️">
Codified by Robert Rhea in 1932, the Six Tenets of Dow Theory outline the universal truths of how markets behave, digest information, and transition between phases.
</ConceptCard>

If you want to understand how institutional money moves markets today, you must understand the rules established over 100 years ago. These six tenets are as applicable to modern crypto markets as they were to 19th-century railroad stocks.

## Tenet 1: The Market Discounts Everything

This is the cornerstone of all technical analysis. Dow Theory posits that every single piece of known information—earnings reports, macroeconomic data, political elections, and human psychology—is immediately factored into the price of the asset.

Therefore, studying the news is futile. By the time a news headline breaks, the "smart money" has already positioned themselves, and the price reflects the event. The only pure truth is the price chart itself.

<Callout type="warn" title="News vs. Price">
Never trade based on how you think a news event *should* affect the market. Trade based on how the market *actually* reacts to it. The market is the only ultimate arbiter of value.
</Callout>

## Tenet 2: The Three Trends (Tides, Waves, Ripples)

Dow likened the market to the ocean.
- **The Primary Trend (The Tide):** Lasts from one to several years. This is the macro bull or bear market.
- **The Secondary Trend (The Wave):** Lasts from three weeks to several months. These are the major retracements (pullbacks) against the primary trend.
- **The Minor Trend (The Ripple):** Lasts less than three weeks. These are daily fluctuations, largely considered "noise" by Dow.

Modern traders apply this exactly the same way using Top-Down Analysis (e.g., Daily for the Tide, 1H for the Wave, 5m for the Ripple).

## Tenet 3: The Three Phases of Primary Trends

Dow identified that massive macro trends do not move in a straight line. They are driven by shifts in psychology and institutional positioning. 

<TrendPhaseVisualizer />

1. **Accumulation Phase:** The market has crashed. Everyone is terrified. Smart money quietly buys shares at deep discounts.
2. **Public Participation Phase:** The trend accelerates. Trend followers jump in. Business conditions improve.
3. **Distribution Phase:** Euphoria sets in. Your taxi driver is buying the stock. Smart money quietly sells their shares to the euphoric public, trapping them at the top.

## Tenet 4: The Averages Must Confirm Each Other

Dow believed that a true bull market could only exist if *both* the Industrial Average and the Transportation Average were making new highs simultaneously. 

If manufacturers are making goods (Industrials rallying), but those goods aren't being shipped (Transports lagging), the economic expansion is a mirage. This concept of cross-asset confirmation is still used today (e.g., comparing Bitcoin to Ethereum, or the S&P 500 to the Nasdaq).

## Tenet 5: Volume Must Confirm the Trend

Dow believed price was the primary indicator, but volume acted as a polygraph test. 
- In a true Bull Market, volume should expand when price rises, and shrink when price falls. 
- If price is rising but volume is drying up, the trend is exhausted and a reversal is imminent. 

<Callout type="tip" title="Modern Volume Application">
While forex and crypto lack centralized volume, we use "Displacement" (large, fast candles creating FVGs) as a modern proxy for institutional volume confirming a trend.
</Callout>

## Tenet 6: A Trend is Assumed in Effect Until a Reversal is Signaled

A body in motion stays in motion. You should always assume the current trend will continue until you have definitive, structural proof that it has reversed. 

This means you do not try to "pick tops" or "catch falling knives." You wait for the market to print a lower high and a lower low (a Change of Character / Break of Structure) before assuming the bear market has begun.

<KeyTakeaways>
- Price discounts all known information; studying price action is superior to trading the news.
- The market moves in three fractal trends (Tide, Wave, Ripple).
- Institutional cycles follow three phases: Accumulation, Participation, Distribution.
- Trends must be confirmed by volume (or displacement).
- Never fight the trend; wait for structural proof of a reversal.
</KeyTakeaways>
