# DXY and Real Yields

Gold does not exist in a vacuum. Because XAUUSD is priced in US Dollars, analyzing the broader dollar index (DXY) and the US Treasury market is mandatory for contextualizing gold's price action.

## The US Dollar Index (DXY)

The DXY measures the value of the US Dollar relative to a basket of foreign currencies (primarily the Euro, Yen, and Pound). 

When the DXY is in a strong uptrend, gold faces structural headwinds. If the dollar is strengthening, it takes fewer dollars to buy an ounce of gold, putting downward pressure on the XAUUSD chart.

<ConceptCard title="The Inverse Correlation">
  While the negative correlation between XAUUSD and DXY is strong, it is not 1-to-1. Sometimes both can rise simultaneously if a global shock causes a flight to safety (investors buying both USD cash and physical Gold).
</ConceptCard>

## The Role of Real Yields

Real yields are arguably the most powerful long-term driver of gold prices.

*Real Yield = Nominal 10-Year Treasury Yield - Expected Inflation*

When real yields are high or rising, holding gold has a high opportunity cost. Capital rotates out of gold ETFs and into bonds. When real yields are negative or falling (inflation outpaces interest rates), gold becomes the preferred store of value.
