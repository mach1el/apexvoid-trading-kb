# The Economic Calendar

For day traders and swing traders in XAUUSD, the economic calendar is just as important as price action. Major data releases inject massive liquidity and volatility into the market, frequently triggering stop hunts and establishing new directional trends.

## Tier 1 Data for Gold Traders

Not all news is created equal. Gold is primarily sensitive to US data that influences the Federal Reserve's monetary policy trajectory.

<ConceptCard title="The Deviation from Consensus">
  Markets price in the expected (consensus) number prior to the release. The volatility occurs when the actual data *deviates* from the consensus. A "good" NFP number might cause gold to rally if the consensus expectation was for an "excellent" number. Trade the deviation, not the absolute figure.
</ConceptCard>

## Pre-News Price Action

Markets often exhibit specific behaviors leading up to major Tier 1 events:
- **Consolidation**: Price compresses into a tight range as institutions sideline their capital, awaiting clarity.
- **Liquidity Building**: Trendlines and clean levels are established, accumulating stops just above and below the current price action.
