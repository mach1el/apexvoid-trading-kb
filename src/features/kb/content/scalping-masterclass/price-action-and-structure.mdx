# Price Action & Market Structure

Price action is the purest form of market data. By reading the narrative behind the candlesticks and mapping the structural shifts, a scalper can determine who is in control of the market at any given moment.

## The Battle Within the Candlestick

Every candlestick represents a struggle between buyers (bulls) and sellers (bears) over a specific time period. The anatomy of a candle—its body, upper wick, and lower wick—tells a story of rejection, continuation, or indecision.

<ConceptCard title="Key Reversal & Momentum Candles">
  - **Hammer (Pin Bar):** A small body with a long lower wick. It indicates that sellers pushed the price down, but buyers overwhelmed them to close near the open. A strong sign of bullish rejection at support.
  - **Shooting Star:** The bearish counterpart to the hammer. A small body with a long upper wick, showing that buyers were rejected at resistance.
  - **Engulfing:** A strong momentum candle where the body completely eclipses the previous candle. A Bullish Engulfing signifies a sudden shift in power to the buyers, while a Bearish Engulfing shows sellers taking aggressive control.
</ConceptCard>

## Sóng (Market Swings): Impulse vs. Correction

The market does not move in straight lines; it moves in waves or swings (Sóng). Understanding the nature of these swings is critical for timing entries.

- **Impulse Swings:** Strong, aggressive moves in the direction of the underlying trend. These consist of large momentum candles.
- **Correction (Pullback) Swings:** Slower, weaker moves against the trend. They typically feature smaller candles and overlapping price action as the market catches its breath.

Scalpers aim to enter at the end of a correction to catch the next impulse swing.

## Identifying Market Structure

Market structure is defined by the sequence of swing highs and swing lows.

<AnnotatedChart showSwingHL={true} />

- **Uptrend (Bullish Structure):** Characterized by Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL). As long as the most recent HL holds, the buyers remain in control.
- **Downtrend (Bearish Structure):** Characterized by Lower Lows (LL) and Lower Highs (LH). As long as the most recent LH is not broken, sellers maintain dominance.
- **Structure Shift:** A trend reversal occurs when the sequence is broken (e.g., a LH is broken in a downtrend, creating a new HH, often referred to as a Break of Structure or BOS).

<Callout type="info" title="Context is King">
  A single candlestick pattern (like a Hammer) is meaningless on its own. It must be traded in the context of market structure—for example, a Hammer forming at a Higher Low (HL) in an established uptrend.
</Callout>
