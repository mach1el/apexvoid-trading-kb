# The Scalper Mindset & AMD Cycle

Successful scalping requires a distinct psychological approach and a deep understanding of market mechanics. Scalpers operate in short timeframes, making quick decisions based on immediate price action rather than long-term fundamentals.

<ConceptCard title="Probabilities over Predictions">
  The cornerstone of a scalper's mindset is focusing on probabilities, not predictions. Instead of trying to guess where the market will go next, professional scalpers execute setups that have a statistical edge. Every trade is just one data point in a larger series. Accept that losses are inevitable and manage them strictly.
</ConceptCard>

## The AMD Cycle: Tracking Smart Money

Institutions and large market participants (often referred to as "Smart Money") leave footprints on the chart. Their activities can generally be categorized into three recurring phases, known as the AMD Cycle.

### 1. Accumulation (A)
This is the phase where smart money builds their positions. The market often moves in a tight, sideways range. Retail traders frequently get chopped up here trying to trade breakouts that fail. For a scalper, this is a period of observation rather than action.

### 2. Manipulation (M)
Also known as the "Judas Swing" or liquidity grab. Price suddenly breaks out of the accumulation range, triggering stop losses and inducing retail traders to enter in the wrong direction. This provides the necessary liquidity for smart money to execute the rest of their orders at optimal prices.

### 3. Distribution (D)
Once smart money has secured their positions, the real move begins. Price expands rapidly in the intended direction. This is the phase where scalpers capitalize, joining the momentum for short-term, high-probability gains.

<Callout type="warning" title="Scalping Gold (XAUUSD)">
  Gold is highly volatile and respects technical levels, making it a favorite for scalpers. However, its rapid movements demand strict risk management. A manipulation phase on XAUUSD can easily wipe out an over-leveraged account before the distribution phase begins. Always use predetermined stop losses and never move them further away as price approaches.
</Callout>
