# High-Probability Chart Patterns

Chart patterns are visual representations of market psychology and the ongoing battle between supply and demand. However, the secret to scalping these patterns isn't trying to predict their formation, but rather trading the confirmed reaction once they complete.

## Classic Scalping Patterns

While there are dozens of patterns, professional scalpers focus on a few high-probability setups that frequently appear on lower timeframes.

<ConceptCard title="Reversal Patterns">
  - **Double Top / Double Bottom:** Represents a failed attempt to break a significant high or low. It shows exhaustion from the dominant side.
  - **Head & Shoulders / Inverse H&S:** A more complex reversal indicating a shift in momentum. The pattern shows an initial push, a deeper push that is ultimately rejected (the head), followed by a final, weaker push (the right shoulder) that fails to reach the extreme.
</ConceptCard>

<ConceptCard title="Continuation Patterns">
  - **Pennants:** Small, symmetrical consolidation patterns that form after a strong impulse move. They resemble a brief pause before the trend resumes.
  - **Triangles (Ascending/Descending):** Indicate a buildup of pressure against a flat support or resistance level. An Ascending Triangle shows buyers making higher lows into resistance, suggesting an eventual upward breakout.
</ConceptCard>

## The Golden Rule: Wait for the Break

The most common mistake retail traders make is trying to anticipate a pattern before it completes. A Double Top is not a Double Top until the support level connecting the two peaks is broken.

<Callout type="danger" title="Don't Guess the Pattern">
  Anticipating a pattern often leads to entering prematurely and getting stopped out. Wait for a decisive candle close beyond the key structural level (the "Neckline"). Confirmation is more valuable than trying to catch the absolute top or bottom.
</Callout>

## The Role Reversal Flip (Break and Retest)

When a key structural level is broken, its role often flips. Previous resistance becomes new support, and previous support becomes new resistance.

This is the optimal entry zone for a scalper. Instead of buying the initial breakout (which can often be a fake-out or manipulation), wait for the price to retest the broken neckline. Look for a rejection candle (like a Hammer or Engulfing) at this new Role Reversal zone to confirm the entry.
