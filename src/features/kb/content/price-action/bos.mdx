{/* 1. Hook */}
A trend is not defined by a moving average or a subjective trendline drawn at an arbitrary angle. A trend is defined by a mechanical, unbroken chain of structural breaks. If you cannot objectively identify a Break of Structure (BOS), you cannot objectively identify the trend.

{/* 2. Definition */}
A **Break of Structure (BOS)** occurs when price successfully closes beyond the preceding major Swing High (in an uptrend) or Swing Low (in a downtrend), signaling that the prevailing trend is continuing.

{/* 3. Deep Explanation */}
## The Mechanics of Trend Continuation

In [Market Structure](/foundations/market-structure), we learned that an uptrend consists of Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL). The moment a new Higher High is confirmed, a Break of Structure has occurred. 

A BOS is the market's way of confirming intent. When price breaks the previous peak, it indicates that demand is still vastly overpowering supply, and institutions are willing to sponsor higher prices. Conversely, in a downtrend, a BOS occurs when the previous Swing Low is broken, confirming that supply remains dominant.

### The Validation Rule: Body Close vs. Wick
The most critical debate in price action trading is *how* a structural level must be broken to be considered a valid BOS. There are two camps, but institutional traders generally adhere to the stricter rule: **The Body Close.**

If a candle pushes above a Swing High but only leaves a wick (tail) above the line, and the *body* of the candle closes back below the line, this is **NOT** a BOS. This is a Liquidity Sweep. The market merely pierced the high to trigger buy-stops (gathering liquidity) before reversing.

A true BOS requires the **body** of the candle to close firmly beyond the structural level. A body close indicates acceptance of the new price level, not just a momentary spike. 

### Internal BOS vs. Swing BOS
Just as there are major and minor swings, there are major and minor structure breaks:
- **Swing BOS (Macro):** A break of the true, major Swing High/Low. This confirms the higher timeframe trend continuation.
- **Internal BOS (Micro):** A break of a minor sub-structure level *inside* the current macro leg. An internal BOS can signal a pullback is ending, but it does not dictate the macro trend.

## How to Map a BOS on a Chart (Step-by-Step)

To keep your charts clean and objective, follow this mapping process:

1. **Locate the Major Swings:** Using the [3-Candle Rule](/key-levels/swing-high-low), identify the most recent valid major Swing High and Swing Low on your chosen timeframe.
2. **Draw the Barrier:** Draw a horizontal ray (line) originating from the extreme wick of the Swing High (if in an uptrend) or Swing Low (if in a downtrend).
3. **Wait for the Interaction:** Monitor price as it approaches the horizontal ray.
4. **Evaluate the Close:** When a candle interacts with the line, wait for the candle to close. 
   - If the candle wicks through the line but the body closes below it, do not label it a BOS. Mark it as an 'X' or a Sweep.
   - If the candle body closes cleanly through the line, label it **BOS**.
5. **Reset:** Once a BOS is confirmed, the lowest point of the pullback that led to the BOS becomes your new protected Swing Low. The trend continues.

{/* 6. Worked Examples */}
## Worked Examples

Let's examine how strict adherence to the body-close rule prevents bad trades.

### Example 1: The True Continuation (Idealized)

**Scenario:** AAPL is in a daily uptrend. The most recent Swing High is exactly $150.00. The recent Swing Low is $142.00.
1. Price rallies from $142.00 and hits $150.00.
2. The daily candle pushes to $152.00. 
3. At the end of the day, the candle closes at $151.50. 
4. The body of the candle has closed completely above the $150.00 level.

**Walkthrough:**
This is a confirmed BOS. The market has accepted prices above the previous peak. The uptrend is validated. The Swing Low at $142.00 is now "protected" (if price were to break below $142.00, the trend would be over). Traders can now look to buy the next pullback.

### Example 2: The Failed Breakout / Sweep (Grounded)

**Scenario:** EUR/USD is in a 1-Hour uptrend. The previous Swing High is 1.0850.
1. Price rallies aggressively toward 1.0850.
2. A large green candle pushes right through the level, hitting 1.0865. Retail breakout traders pile in.
3. Over the next 45 minutes, massive selling pressure enters the market. 
4. The 1-Hour candle closes as a bearish pin-bar. The body of the candle closes at 1.0840 (below the 1.0850 line).

**Walkthrough:**
Retail traders who bought the moment price crossed 1.0850 assumed a BOS was occurring. However, because the candle body failed to close above the line, the BOS is invalid. This was a Liquidity Sweep. Institutions used the breakout buyers as liquidity to fill short orders. Price subsequently collapses. Waiting for the candle close is the ultimate filter.

## Modern Framework Translations

- **SMC/ICT:** The term BOS is universally used in SMC to denote trend continuation. It is directly contrasted against a [CHoCH (Change of Character)](/price-action/choch), which denotes a trend *reversal*.
- **Dow Theory:** Charles Dow referred to this simply as the confirmation of a trend (higher highs confirming higher lows). The core tenet that "a trend is assumed to be in effect until it gives a definitive signal of reversal" relies entirely on successive BOS formations.
- **Classic Breakout Trading:** Traditional traders call a BOS a "Breakout." However, pure price action traders differentiate between a BOS (structural) and a Breakout (which can occur from diagonal trendlines or arbitrary chart patterns).

{/* 9. FAQ */}
## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need a full candle body to close above the line, or just the close price?**
A: Just the close price of the candle needs to be above the horizontal line. However, a BOS is considered much "stronger" and higher probability if the entire body (or a large portion of it) closes above the line, showing strong displacement.

**Q: If the 1-Hour candle sweeps the high, but the 15-Minute candle closes above it, is it a BOS?**
A: This depends entirely on what timeframe you are mapping. If you are mapping the 1-Hour structure, you *must* wait for the 1-Hour candle close. A 15-Minute close is irrelevant to the 1-Hour structure. Mixing timeframes mid-analysis is a recipe for disaster. 

**Q: How many BOS do I need to confirm a trend?**
A: A minimum of two. One break of a high could just be a deep liquidity run. Two successive breaks of structure (creating a HH, HL, HH sequence) definitively confirm the trend is established.
