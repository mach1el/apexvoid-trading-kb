Retail traders move the market in inches; institutions move it in miles. If you want to trade alongside the "Smart Money," you must learn to recognize the exact moment they enter the market with massive volume. That footprint is called Displacement.

{/* 2. Definition */}
**Displacement** is a sudden, aggressive move in price characterized by large, wide-range candles that close near their extremes. This violent movement often leaves behind an **Imbalance (Fair Value Gap)**, which acts as a magnetic target for future price action.

{/* 3. Deep Explanation */}
## The Mechanics of Institutional Volume

When a massive financial institution needs to buy 100,000 contracts of an asset, they cannot simply click "Market Buy." There is not enough liquidity at the current price to fill that order, so their buy order will eat through the entire order book, rocketing the price upward. 

When you see a massive, full-bodied green candle (or a series of them) suddenly appear on a chart, you are witnessing this exact phenomenon. This is **Displacement**. It is the undeniable footprint that smart money has stepped into the market.

### What is an Imbalance (FVG)?

Because Displacement happens so violently and quickly, the market is not given a chance to efficiently match buyers and sellers. It creates a vacuum or a gap in price delivery. 

In a perfectly efficient market, the wicks of consecutive candles overlap slightly, ensuring every price point was traded by both buyers and sellers. When displacement occurs, a three-candle sequence will often print where the wick of Candle 1 and the wick of Candle 3 do not touch, leaving a gap across the body of Candle 2. 

This gap is an **Imbalance**, most commonly known today as a **Fair Value Gap (FVG)**.

### Why Price Returns to the FVG

The algorithmic pricing models used by central banks and major institutions are designed to maintain market efficiency. When a massive FVG is left behind, it represents an inefficiency. 

Therefore, algorithms will actively drag price back to that gap to "repair" the inefficiency, allowing traders who missed the initial move to transact at those skipped prices. Once the gap is repaired (mitigated), the original institutional trend resumes.

## How to Map Imbalances (Step-by-Step)

To find highly actionable trade entries, follow this process:

1. **Find the Displacement:** Look for a massive, full-bodied candle that breaks a structural level (a BOS or CHoCH). If the candle is small or has huge wicks, it is not displacement.
2. **Isolate the 3-Candle Formation:** Look at the massive candle (Candle 2). Now, look at the candle immediately before it (Candle 1) and immediately after it (Candle 3).
3. **Draw the Gap:** 
   - *Bullish FVG:* Draw a box from the High of Candle 1 to the Low of Candle 3. 
   - *Bearish FVG:* Draw a box from the Low of Candle 1 to the High of Candle 3.
4. **Extend the Zone:** Drag that box to the right. This is your Fair Value Gap (Imbalance) zone.
5. **Wait for the Retest:** Set an alert at the edge of the box. Do not chase the displacement. Wait patiently for price to pull back and enter the box.
6. **Execute:** Once price enters the FVG, drop to a lower timeframe and look for a reversal pattern (like a CHoCH) to enter the market in the direction of the original displacement.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: The Perfect Mitigation (Idealized)

**Scenario:** The S&P 500 has been chopping sideways for hours. 
1. Suddenly, a massive 15-Minute green candle prints, breaking a major resistance level. 
2. Candle 1 had a High of 4100. Candle 3 has a Low of 4110. Candle 2 (the massive displacement candle) fills the space between.
3. You draw a box between 4100 and 4110. This is the FVG.
4. For the next hour, price slowly drifts down, eventually tagging 4105 (right in the middle of the FVG).
5. The moment it taps the FVG, it aggressively rejects and rockets to 4150.

**Walkthrough:**
This is the holy grail of Smart Money Concepts. The initial massive candle was the institutions showing their hand (bullish). The slow drift down was the algorithms repairing the inefficiency. The tap of the FVG was the opportunity for smart money to add to their position before sending price higher.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does price HAVE to return to the FVG?**
A: No. In extremely strong, runaway trends (like a crypto bull market), price may leave FVGs behind for weeks or months. However, in normal, two-way markets, FVGs are usually filled within the same session or the next.

**Q: Do I enter at the start of the FVG, or the middle?**
A: It depends on your risk tolerance. Aggressive traders enter the moment price touches the edge of the FVG. Conservative traders wait for price to hit the 50% mark of the FVG (called the Consequent Encroachment) to secure a better risk-to-reward ratio.

**Q: What if price closes completely beyond the FVG?**
A: If a candle body closes completely outside the FVG, the setup is invalidated. The FVG has failed to hold as support/resistance, and the opposing side is now in control.
